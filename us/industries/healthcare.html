Overview

Healthcare & Physician Practices

A medical or dental practice earns like a business and is taxed like a gauntlet: professional-entity rules that vary by state, owner compensation balanced against QBI limits written specifically for professions like yours, retirement plans that are often the largest deduction available, and equipment purchases that deserve a depreciation strategy rather than an afterthought.

We work with practice owners and the physicians behind them — entity returns, personal returns, quarterly planning — and, for internationally trained physicians, the cross-border layer too: foreign assets reported, relocations planned, and both countries' obligations kept straight.

What we do for you

Practice & Owner Returns

Entity and personal returns prepared together — PC, PLLC, or S-corp — so nothing falls between them.

Compensation & QBI Planning

Owner salary and distribution strategy worked against the QBI deduction's professional-services limits.

Retirement Plan Strategy

401(k), cash-balance, and defined-benefit design coordination — frequently the practice's biggest deduction.

Practice Accounting

Bookkeeping and monthly reporting on QuickBooks Online — collections, overhead, and provider-level performance visible.

Equipment & Depreciation

Section 179 and bonus depreciation planning for equipment and build-outs — timed to the tax year, not the invoice.

International Physicians

Cross-border reporting (FBAR, foreign assets) and pre-arrival planning for internationally trained providers.

Overview

E-Commerce & Retail

E-commerce outgrew its tax rules years ago. Economic nexus means a seller can owe registration in states it has never set foot in; marketplace facilitator laws collect some of the tax but not all of it; and inventory placed in third-party warehouses quietly creates footprint. Meanwhile the books must reconcile payouts, fees, returns, and inventory across every channel.

We build e-commerce accounting that closes cleanly — channel settlements reconciled, COGS and inventory tracked properly — and manage the sales-tax layer proactively: nexus reviewed on a schedule, registrations filed as thresholds approach, not after they've passed.
